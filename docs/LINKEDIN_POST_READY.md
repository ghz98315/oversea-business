$80,000. That's what a US startup almost lost last week.

They found an "EV charging cable manufacturer" online. Great price. UL 2251 certified. 5,000 MOQ. Ready to ship.

Red flags everywhere:

❌ The UL certificate? Photoshopped. Wrong factory name, expired 2 years ago.

Here's what most buyers miss:

→ Real EV charging factories have cable extrusion lines, not just assembly benches
→ UL certificates have verifiable numbers in UL's database
→ Automotive-grade requirements are completely different from consumer electronics

That startup? I connected them with a verified factory in Dongguan. Real certifications, actual capacity, proper testing equipment. They placed their order last week.
